# NURSING AUSBILDUNG IN GERMANY — COMPLETE GUIDE FOR BEGINNERS

## 1. WHAT IS PFLEGEAUSBILDUNG (NURSING TRAINING)?

The nursing training (Pflegeausbildung) in Germany is a state-recognized vocational training
that qualifies graduates as certified nursing professionals (Pflegefachmann/Pflegefachfrau).
Since the 2020 Nursing Reform Act (Pflegeberufegesetz), the three former nursing professions
have been unified into one comprehensive qualification covering:
- General nursing (Gesundheits- und Krankenpflege)
- Elderly care nursing (Altenpflege)
- Pediatric nursing (Kinderkrankenpflege)

## 2. DURATION AND STRUCTURE

- Total Duration: 3 years (36 months)
- Theory Hours: Minimum 2,100 hours at nursing school (Pflegeschule)
- Practical Hours: Minimum 2,500 hours in various care settings
- Practical Placements include:
  * Acute care hospital (Krankenhaus)
  * Long-term care / nursing home (Pflegeheim)
  * Outpatient / home care (ambulante Pflege)
  * Pediatric care (Kinderklinik)
  * Psychiatric care (Psychiatrie)

## 3. ENTRY REQUIREMENTS

- Educational level: Secondary school certificate (Mittlere Reife/Realschulabschluss)
  OR basic school leaving certificate (Hauptschulabschluss) plus completed nursing assistant training
- Health fitness certificate from a doctor
- Extended criminal record check (erweitertes Führungszeugnis)
- German language proficiency: Minimum B2 level (C1 recommended for patient care)
- Minimum age: 18 years (17 with special permission)

## 4. LANGUAGE REQUIREMENTS

German language proficiency is critical for nursing in Germany.

Why B2/C1 is required:
- Patient Communication: Nurses must explain procedures, ask about symptoms, provide comfort
- Medical Documentation: All nursing care must be documented in written German
- Team Communication: Doctors, therapists, and colleagues communicate in German
- Emergency Situations: Fast, accurate communication can save lives
- Medication Safety: Reading prescriptions and medication instructions correctly

Accepted language certificates:
- Goethe-Zertifikat B2 / C1
- telc Deutsch B2 / C1 Pflege (specialized healthcare language certificate)
- TestDaF
- ÖSD Certificate

If you don't have B2 yet: Many hospitals and care facilities offer language preparation programs.
Some Ausbildung providers accept students with B2 and support them to reach C1 during training.

## 5. SALARY DURING TRAINING (Ausbildungsgehalt)

Training salary increases each year:
- Year 1: approximately €1,100 – €1,200 gross/month
- Year 2: approximately €1,150 – €1,270 gross/month
- Year 3: approximately €1,250 – €1,400 gross/month

Important notes:
- Public institutions (e.g., university hospitals) typically pay according to TVöD collective agreement
- Private institutions may offer different rates
- Training salary is taxed — net amount will be lower
- Additional benefits: some employers offer meal subsidies, transport allowances

After qualifying:
- Starting salary as a qualified nurse: approximately €2,800 – €3,500 gross/month
- With specialized training and experience: up to €4,500 gross/month
- Night shifts, weekends, holidays: 20–35% pay supplements (Zuschläge)

## 6. APPLICATION PROCESS

Step 1: Research — Find a nursing school (Pflegeschule) and a training hospital/care facility
Step 2: Prepare your application documents:
  - German CV (Lebenslauf — tabular format)
  - Motivational letter (Motivationsschreiben)
  - School certificates with certified German translation
  - Health certificate from doctor
  - Criminal record check (Führungszeugnis)
  - Language certificate (B2 minimum)
Step 3: Submit application (online or by post)
Step 4: Interview (Vorstellungsgespräch — usually in German!)
Step 5: Sign training contract (Ausbildungsvertrag)
Step 6: Start training — typically in October or April each year

## 7. THE NURSING SCHOOL CURRICULUM

Topics taught in nursing school:
- Anatomy and Physiology: Structure and function of the human body
- Pathology: Disease processes and conditions
- Nursing Science: Evidence-based nursing theories and models
- Pharmacology: Medication types, effects, and safe administration
- Hygiene and Infection Control: Hand hygiene, sterile techniques, isolation
- Communication and Patient Counseling: Therapeutic communication skills
- Ethics in Nursing: Patient rights, dignity, end-of-life care
- Legal Framework: Liability, medical confidentiality (Schweigepflicht)
- First Aid and Emergency Care: BLS, CPR basics
- Wound Care: Wound assessment and dressing techniques
- Nursing Documentation: Digital and paper-based documentation systems

## 8. EXAMINATIONS (STAATSEXAMEN)

The training concludes with state examinations:

Written Examination:
- 3 supervised written papers (Klausuren), 120 minutes each
- Topics drawn from all nursing care areas

Oral Examination:
- Discussion with examination board
- Case-based questions and scenarios

Practical Examination:
- Real nursing situation with an actual patient
- Planning, execution, and reflection of nursing care

Upon passing: You receive the state-recognized title of Pflegefachmann/Pflegefachfrau

## 9. RECOGNITION OF FOREIGN NURSING QUALIFICATIONS (ANERKENNUNG)

If you already have a nursing qualification from another country, you may be able to get it
recognized in Germany instead of completing the full Ausbildung.

The Anerkennung (Recognition) Process:
1. Contact the competent authority in the German state (Bundesland) where you want to work
2. Submit documents: diploma, transcripts, proof of work experience (with certified German translations)
3. The authority assesses whether your qualification is equivalent to the German standard
4. If there are gaps: You may need to complete a "Compensation Measure" (Ausgleichsmaßnahme):
   - Aptitude test (Eignungsprüfung) OR
   - Adaptation period / internship (Anpassungslehrgang — typically 6 months)
5. After successful completion: You receive German nursing recognition

Countries with simplified recognition processes:
- EU/EEA countries: Simplified procedure under EU Directive 2005/36/EC
- Non-EU countries: Full assessment required (can take 6–12 months)

Important: Recognition is state-specific. If you move to a different German state,
you may need to re-register, but not repeat the recognition process.

## 10. DAILY ROUTINE OF A NURSING STUDENT (TYPICAL HOSPITAL SHIFT)

Early Shift (Frühschicht: 06:30 – 14:30):
06:30 — Handover (Übergabe): Receive information from night shift about each patient
07:00 — Morning care: Help patients with washing, dressing, oral hygiene
07:30 — Breakfast: Assist patients who need help eating
08:00 — Medication rounds: Administer prescribed medications with supervision
09:00 — Medical rounds (Visite): Accompany doctors, take notes, assist
10:00 — Nursing procedures: Wound dressings, blood draws, catheter care, IV monitoring
12:00 — Lunch assistance for patients
13:00 — Documentation: Record all nursing activities in patient records
14:00 — Afternoon handover: Brief next shift on patient status
14:30 — Shift ends

Late Shift (Spätschicht: 14:00 – 22:00): Similar responsibilities, fewer procedures
Night Shift (Nachtschicht: 22:00 – 06:30): Monitoring, emergency response, less frequent care

## 12. TIPS FOR STARTING YOUR AUSBILDUNG

1. Practice German EVERY day — use medical vocabulary flashcard apps (e.g., Anki)
2. Get your work uniform and nursing shoes before your first day
3. Punctuality is extremely important in Germany — never be late
4. Ask questions — your Praxisanleiter (practical supervisor) is there to help you
5. Always document nursing actions — if it's not written down, it didn't happen
6. Practice strict hygiene — hand hygiene is non-negotiable
7. Form study groups with fellow students for exam preparation
8. Take care of your own health — rest, nutrition, and mental wellbeing matter
9. Build a Fachbuch (professional reference book) collection for your specialty
10. Know your rights: You are entitled to holidays, sick pay, and supervision
